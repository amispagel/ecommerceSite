The Growth of Mobile E-Commerce and Analytics

    People have been using mobile devices more and more each day, revealing the growing need to focus business resources into creating a more mobile-friendly way to connect. While there is still a reluctance to learn these new technologies, it does not change the fact that more people are now connecting to websites via mobile devices than desktops.1 Also, as technology becomes cheaper, that’s one more reason to take the leap and give mobile devices a chance.

    In the age of data, competition has grown rapidly to perfect the art of gathering and utilizing big data. Gaining the competitive edge means storing all the data that comes in contact with your business, and using it, too. There’s a myriad of new ways of gathering data, one being actually recording what users are doing on your websites. A popular company that started up in 2006, Clicktale, does this, but takes it one step farther. It also creates reports for you that summarize the data, and also allows you to filter all your recordings based on different things, like which form was used the most before users left, or which field was left blank the most while submitting a form.2 To put into perspective how big analytics has become, Clicktale grew by 5,553% between 2006 and 2011. Mouseflow is another company that records heat maps and user sessions, and it provides a demo on their website to see what the video would look like.

    One of the biggest concerns for consumers is the security of their information. It’s even more concerning with how easily your phone can be stolen. Apple has come up with the fingerprint identification, and Google has created the Google Wallet that uses a pin. This has provided more of a sense of security, but there are also tons of payment companies that offer to be the man in the middle and offer frameworks to easily implement their payment systems on your website and mobile apps. All you have to do to insert the payment system is create an identification key for your business, then insert the html form into your web page.

    There are many statistics proving why it is beneficial to focus resources into mobile development. According to Linda Bustos from Infographic1, those who shop via mobile spend 66% more than those who only buy in store. According to eMarketer, 63.2% of Millennials used mobile devices to get online in 2012. Even more concerning is that Millennials will make up 75% of the global workforce by 2025. People want an integrated, seamless experience with shopping, regardless of the channel. A new app called Spring offers a photo feed of products, with a multitude of brands, luxury labels, and emerging designers. It has taken the ease of buying on mobile to a new level by getting rid of the shopping cart. All you need to do to buy something is put your payment information into the app once, then click on a product and swipe. You can even follow your favorite brands and product lines with the app. New innovations like this will make buying on mobile more attractive. Beacons, or Low Energy Bluetooth transmitters, are signals that can be detected by smartphones or tablets. These can help in store shopping by creating a personalized and contextual experience. By knowing what products you like, analytics can help a business remind you of a similar product within a store by sending a beacon to your phone when you’re nearby the product. This way, mobile can even enhance the in store shopping experience.
